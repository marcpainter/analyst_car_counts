Informative Value of Diverse Information

To function well, markets require participants to uncover and incorporate diverse information (Hayek 1945; Goldstein and Yang 2015)

For financial markets, much info is disseminated widely

  • Annual reports
  • Earnings calls

Other important firm information is geographically dispersed and not equally accessible

Some local information is representative, some is noisy

  • "Inflation and supply shortages mean a return of empty shelves and panic buying." Bloomberg

  • "Grocers, shoppers cope with power outages." Dallas Morning News

Analysts and local information

  • Local info is valuable to market participants (Van Nieuwerburgh and Veldkamp, 2009)
    • Analysts (especially in retail) often highlight store visits as part of their primary research
  • As more diverse local information is incorporated, idiosyncratic noise from local markets are averaged away.
    • But... sell-side analysts are geographically concentrated

Research questions

  • Do financial analysts of retail companies incorporate local firm information into their forecasts?
    • YES! Retail analysts shade earnings forecasts toward their locally observed parking lot car counts
  • Are there implications of analysts' geographic diversity for the market?
    • YES! Consensus forecast errors are larger and firm liquidity is lower when analysts are more geographically concentrated
    • The market values forecasts from geographically unique analysts and prices are more informative for firms with dispersed analysts

Analyst forecasting is a nice setting

  • Markets rely on analyst forecasts as a significant source of cash flow information (Kothari et al, 2016)
  • Nature of information
    • A large fraction of firm information is available to all analysts (earnings calls and EDGAR)
    • Local firm performance may vary across stores for same firm at same point in time
      • Prior research has had to rely on time-invariant measures (distance to HQ) or non-firm specific proxies (state GDP)
  • Identification - many agents predicting the same event and also those agents are predicting other events at same time

A new lense on the importance of geographically dispersed info

  • Information environment and knowledge production is different for firms with operations in many states relative to firms with few (Garcia and Norli 2012; Bernile et al 2015)
  • Market is slow in diffusing this info (Addoum et al 2017)

Our setting allows us to isolate firm-specific local information. We can document its influence on analyst forecasts and test whether theoretical models on diverse info (Goldstein and Yang 2015) hold true in this setting.

Novel setting to identify influences on analyst forecasts

  • Ethnic diversity of analysts (Merkley et al, 2019) Career concerns (Harford et al, 2019) Social interaction between analysts- (Chen et al, 2018) Weather (Dehaan et al, 2017) Industry co-location (Jennings et al, 2017)

Empirical challenge

How to identify and observe geographically diverse local firm information?
  • Historically not observable

    • Solution - Orbital Insight

    • Daily car count data for individual store locations
      • 5.4 million parking lot observations
      • 176,935 unique U.S. store locations
    • Proxy for overall local firm info (talks with managers, foot traffic)
    • 138 retailers (>50% of US retail)
      • Nationwide firms like Walmart, Home Depot, and Starbucks
      • Regional chains like Bob Evans and Ollie's Bargain Outlet
      • 2009-2015
        • not available to market for most of sample

    Orbital Insight image conversion process

    • OI partners with a variety of satellite providers
    • Satellites pass a given latitude the same time every day
      • High resolution satellites - roughly 50 square cm/pixel
    • Car counts are normalized to reflect historical traffic patterns associated with time of day and day of week
    • Not every store is photographed every day
    • Normalizing based on historical distribution allows OI to account for store quality (e.g., standard Walmart vs. Super Walmart)

    Aggregate vs. Local Car Counts

    • Aggregate satellite imagery is predictive of firm performance (Zhu 2019; Katona et al 2019)
    • However, local car counts are a very noisy measure of the aggregate

    Our application of satellite image data

    • Identify financial analyst location using data obtained through FOIA requests to state regulators
      • Available from Uniform Application for Securities Industry Regulation or Transfer (Form U4)
      • Match to IBES sample by name and career history
    • Disaggregate satellite data to derive local, time-varying measures of firm performance

    \(Car\ count\ \mathit{difference}_{j,m,q} = \frac{Avg\ Local\ car \ count_{j,m,q} - Avg\ National \ car \ count_{j,q}}{Avg\ National \ car \ count_{j,q}}\)

      • An analyst in MSA \(m\) in quarter \(q\) only observes the local car count for firm \(j\)
      • Mean Car Count Difference: 0.17 Std Dev: 0.46
      • Isolates the noisy local information

    What are the market implications?

    • Noise from local info should aggregate when analysts are clustered and cancel out when analysts are disperse.
    • Expand sample to full set of IBES firms
    • Firms w/ more geographically concentrated analysts will have
      • Higher consensus forecast errors
      • Lower liquidity, due to a deteriorated information environment (Goldstein and Yang 2015)

    Analyst geographic dispersion and price informativeness

    Theoretical models like Goldstein and Yang (2015) suggest that a firm’s stock price will be more informative about future cash flows when the information that investors can access is more diverse.

    We test whether more geographically diverse analysts is related to more informative prices using a future earnings response coefficient model (Kothari and Sloan 1992; Israeli et al. 2017; Zhu 2019)

    \(Ret_{j,t} = \beta_1*Earn_{j,t+1} + \beta_2*Disperse_{j,t} + \beta_3*Disperse_{j,t}\times Earn_{j,t+1} \\ + \sum\limits_{k}\beta_k*Controls_{j,t} + \gamma’*FE + \epsilon_{j,t}\)

  • \(j\) indexes firm, \(t\) indexes year

    • Prices are more informative when analysts are disperse

    Conclusion

    Analysts rely on local firm information, which affects consensus forecasts and firm liquidity due to variation in analyst geographic diversity

    Investors value dispersed analysts, and prices are more informative when analysts are geographically diverse
