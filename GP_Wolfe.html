Two Nobel prize winners go to a county fair...

Friedrich Hayek

  • Wisdom of the crowd: markets will aggregate this information even if each agent possesses a small fraction of the sum total of knowledge

Daniel Kahneman

  • Individuals often generalize from small amounts of personally observed data
  • The errors of the individuals must be independent for the crowd to be wise

Sell-side analysts are geographically concentrated

  • Top 20 MSAs for analysts in our retail firm sample
  • Analysts often highlight store visits as part of their primary research

Research questions

  • Do financial analysts of retail firms make hasty generalizations based on their own local information?
  • What is the implication of analysts' geographic diversity for the market?

Analyst forecasting is a nearly ideal setting

  • High stakes - career incentives
  • Markets rely on analyst forecasts as a significant source of cash flow information (Kothari et al, 2016)
  • Nature of information
    • A significant fraction of firm information is available to all analysts e.g. earnings calls and EDGAR
    • For nationwide retail firms, local firm performance may vary across stores at same point in time
    • Identification - many agents predicting the same event and also those agents are predicting other events at same time
  • Setting is also a novel way to identify how analysts make decisions

Empirical challenge

How to identify and observe geographically diverse local information?

Solution(s)

  • Heliosynchronous satellite imagery
    • Orbital Insight provides parking lot car counts of retail firms
    • Available across all U.S. major metropolitan areas
  • Detailed financial analyst location data
    • Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests

Research findings

  • Satellite imagery sample: analysts shade earnings forecasts toward their locally observed parking lot car counts
  • IBES sample: consensus forecast errors are larger when analysts are more concentrated in fewer MSAs

Our application of satellite image data

  • Disaggregate satellite data to derive local, time-varying measures of firm performance

\(Car\ count\ \mathit{difference}_{j,m,q} = \frac{Local\ car \ count_{j,m,q} - National \ car \ count_{j,q}}{National \ car \ count_{j,q}}\)

    • Firm \(j\) in MSA \(m\) in quarter \(q\)
    • Isolates the noisy local information
  • Match with financial analyst location data obtained through FOIA requests to state regulators
    • Available from Uniform Application for Securities Industry Regulation or Transfer (Form U4)

Heterogeneity in analyst local information generalization - Findings

  • Analysts with more experience are less likely to generalize based on local information
  • Analysts are less likely to generalize local information when analysts in other MSAs also cover the firm
    • Analysts are not less likely to generalize local information when other analysts cover the firm but are located in the same MSA

Addressing endogeneity

Endogeneity is not an obvious concern

  • brokerage firms unlikely to shift operations in response to the relative geographic dispersion of any single firm

To rule out any potential concern, we exploit the exogenous shift in geographic coverage due to brokerage closures and mergers

  • Hong and Kacperczyk (2010), Kelly and Ljungqvist (2012)

Look within firms that lose coverage due to exogenous reduction in analyst coverage

  • Some firms will see decreased geographic diversity (e.g., lose only analyst in Minneapolis)
  • Others will retain the same geographic diversity (e.g., lose one of three analysts in New York)

Conclusion

Analysts generalize based on salient local information

In the aggregate, we find geographic diversity improves the consensus forecast
